# Expert Lens and Verification Playbook

Use this guide to choose perspectives and pressure-test conclusions.

## Lens Selection

Pick 3 to 5 lenses from these categories:

- Domain specialist
- Historian or precedent analyst
- Operator or implementer
- Economist or incentive analyst
- User or stakeholder advocate
- Policy, ethics, or governance lens
- Skeptical engineer or devil's advocate

Always include one skeptical lens.

## Skeptical Lens Questions

- Which premise is weakest?
- What evidence would falsify the leading claim?
- What has been ignored because it is inconvenient?
- What breaks at scale, under stress, or in edge cases?
- What would a critic say is naive, overstated, or underspecified?

## Conceptual Blending Prompts

Use these when generating non-obvious alternatives:

- Blend the problem with ecology or evolution.
- Blend the problem with logistics or supply chains.
- Blend the problem with game design or behavioral incentives.
- Blend the problem with safety engineering or reliability design.
- Blend the problem with urban planning, architecture, or networks.

The blend should improve the solution, not just decorate it.

## Verification Pass

For the draft, ask:

1. What are the 3 to 7 most important factual claims?
2. What evidence supports each?
3. Which claims are inference rather than direct evidence?
4. What counterexample would most damage the conclusion?
5. What minimal revision would make the answer more accurate?

## Final Standard

The final answer should be:

- deeper than a summary
- more honest than a sales pitch
- more structured than a brainstorm
- more useful than a literature dump
